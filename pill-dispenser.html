Junior Design
August 2023-May 2024

This pill dispenser will accurately dispense 30 pills (used BBs to simulate pills) no matter how much pills are stored.

Background

For junior design, I along with a team of 9 were tasked with filling 12 bottles with 30 pills each. We engineered a pill dispenser that, while a small component of the overall project, is a compelling machine on its own.

Iteration

(Talk about first double gate hand operated pill dispenser)

The concept was then reiterated to use a gear in order to shuttle the pills down a hole. A limit switch was installed to count how many times the gear has moved via the physical pressing of the limit switch with the gear tooth. It has code to sync up with the rotary table and to stop dispensing once the limit switch detects thirty gear clicks which indicates all thirty pills dispensed.

Wiring diagram of the pill dispenser.

The actual circuitry

Although, the limit switch had a 100% success rate at dispensing 30 pills, a problem arose where when the pill dispenser is near empty, not every gear tooth will hold a pill, but it still gets counted any way, affecting how many pills go into the last few bottles. So the limit switch was replaced to a break beam sensor, using the physical breaking of a beam to count how many pills were dispensed.

Wiring diagram of the pill dispenser.

By itself, the pill dispenser was able to accurately dispense 30 pills even when not all of the gear teeth were full, but unfortunately, it wasn't well synced with the rotary table, leading to premature dispensing when there wasn't a bottle underneath.

So fast forward 2 years, I have synced the pill dispenser to start dispensing after a certain amount of rotary table rotations and then it will start dispensing.

Conclusion

The motor controller was changed to a simpler system that was easier to program and use compared to the first iteration. The people who tried using the motor controller could instantly figure out how to use it.
